The Problem

You missed it.
Again.

When we suppress an emotion, our face 'leaks' for about 50 milliseconds. That's faster than you can blink.

  • The Subtle Motion: It's often just a twitch of 1.5 pixels.
  • Why it matters: It tells us what you're actually feeling, not what you're saying.
  • The Gap: Humans catch only 10% of these. AI needs to do better.

Our Solution

The Secret Sauce

We don't just look at the face; we track it in three different ways at once. It's like having three experts talking to each other.

The Brain (ML)

Meet the TCN.

Instead of reading video one frame at a time, our TCN looks at the whole clip at once. No sequential bottlenecks.

  • Dilated Convolutions: It's like having a zoom lens on time.
  • Residual Logic: It doesn't 'forget' the beginning of the clip by the time it reaches the end.
  • Speed: It's parallel. It's snappy. It's just better.
The Face (DIP)

Logic
Meets Anatomy

You can't just look at 'random' pixels. You have to know where the muscles are pulling.

We use Action Units (AUs) to gate the AI's attention to the parts of the face that actually move during stress.

  • Eyebrows: The 'Surprise' trigger.
  • Lip Corners: The 'Hidden Joy' leak.
  • Nose Bridge: The 'Disgust' signal.

The Math

Tracking
